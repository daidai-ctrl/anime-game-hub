Anime Vanguards Update 12.5 "Turning Tides" arrived on May 9, 2026, and it's the most substantial content patch the game has received this year. The developers completely rebuilt the Odyssey mode, added six powerful new units, introduced a permanent Skill Tree progression system, overhauled the guild infrastructure, and addressed over 300 community-reported bugs. If you've been away from the game or need to catch up on everything new, this guide covers everything you need to know.

## What's New in Update 12.5

### Six New Units

The headline additions in this update are six new characters spanning multiple banners and event content:

**Shinobi God (Infinite Dreams)** — This Madara-inspired Vanguard is the Spring Banner's marquee unit. He brings massive AOE damage, crowd control capabilities, and utility that makes him instantly viable for both new and veteran players.

**Time Goddess (Crazed)** — Kurumi enters via the World Line banner with a unique time-manipulation kit. She synergizes exceptionally well with slow-based team compositions while dealing surprisingly high damage output.

**Strongest Shinobi (Martial)** — This Might Guy-inspired unit is the Spring Event's marquee reward. You need to survive to Wave 100 in the Eternal Tyrant survival mode for a chance at obtaining him (20% drop rate). Once evolved into his Martial form, he becomes one of the strongest DPS units currently available.

**Jiraiya** — The standard event unit obtainable through farming event currency. He functions as a support DPS hybrid with nullify application, strong slows, and burn damage.

**Fruit Eater (He Wins)** — Inspired by Turles, this unit is surprisingly underrated. His damage output becomes insane once fully stacked, and he provides excellent slowing effects similar to field-control units.

**Devoted Demon (Obsessed)** — An Obito-inspired unit featuring unique phasing mechanics that set it apart from other additions.

## Complete Odyssey Mode Rework

The Odyssey rework represents the biggest gameplay change in this update. The old linear stage system is gone, replaced by a roguelike progression structure with branching paths.

### How the New Odyssey Works

The new Odyssey Adventure mode features a map system where you choose your route through different node types:

- **Shop Nodes** — Spend resources on upgrades and cards
- **Boss Rooms** — Challenging encounters that act as checkpoints (last 15 waves)
- **Elite Rooms** — High-value combat encounters rewarding powerful cards

The core innovation is the card system. As you progress through runs, you collect cards that modify your characters' abilities, creating character-based build progression. Your Shinobi God might play completely differently depending on which cards you've collected.

### Best Starter Characters

For beginners, Ragna remains the easiest and strongest choice for early Odyssey progression. His damage scales extremely hard in later floors, though the early game can feel difficult until you obtain his key unit cards.

Key cards to prioritize for Ragna:
- **Monarch's Breakthrough** — Increases placement count
- **All Range Rage** — Grants Full AOE attacks
- **Elite Conquest** — Extra placements after defeating elite bosses (available around Rank 4)

### Unit Ranking System

Odyssey Adventure has separate ranking systems for every character. Each unit levels independently through wave progression, rewarding dedicated farming. Reaching Rank 10 takes considerable time but unlocks substantial rerolls and Memoria materials.

## Elemental Tower Skill Tree

The new Skill Tree provides permanent progression for long-term players. You earn skill points by clearing Elemental Tower floors, with each stat node capping at Level 50.

### Three Skill Tree Branches

- **Damage Tree** — Damage boosts, burn scaling, and elite buffs
- **Range Tree** — Range boosts, slow effects, and targeting improvements
- **Cost Tree** — Cost reduction, refunds, and income boosts

The damage nodes provide the most immediate value, while the cost reduction path pays off heavily in harder content. Maxing all branches earns you the "Conqueror of All Elements" title. Testing showed a DPS increase of approximately 18-22% between zero skill points and fully invested damage paths.

The progression requires clearing 1,390 total floors, with a reset option available for 100,000 Gold.

## Guild System Overhaul

Guilds now operate on a level 1-20 system with proper infrastructure:

- **Weekly Buffs** — Rotate based on guild level from donation goals
- **Donation Tracks** — Gold, Gems, and Guild Coins for shared benefits
- **Activity Points** — Track individual participation
- **Weekly Guild Boss** — Lord of Shadows challenges guilds together
- **Rankings** — Competitive element pitting guilds against each other

If you're not in an active guild, now is the ideal time to find one. Organized groups have real incentives to coordinate.

## Spring Event

The Spring Event runs a survival-style format with endless progression. There is no final wave — you keep going until your team gets overwhelmed. Longer survival means more event currency for the Spring Shop.
