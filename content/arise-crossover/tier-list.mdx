## Arise Crossover Tier List 2026 — Complete Shadow Rankings

Welcome to our comprehensive Arise Crossover tier list for 2026. In this game, Shadows serve as your core combat companions—powerful entities that deal damage, provide utility, and help you conquer dungeons, bosses, and PvP battles. With the recent Ghoul Battle Pass introducing three game-changing HGM+ Shadows, the meta has shifted dramatically. This guide ranks every essential Shadow from **S-tier** (absolute must-haves) to **D-tier** (avoid investing), so you know exactly where to focus your resources.

## S-Tier Shadows — Absolute Must-Haves

S-Tier Shadows represent the pinnacle of power in Arise Crossover. These units deliver overwhelming damage, game-changing abilities, and exceptional value across all game modes. If you're serious about progressing, these should be your top priority.

### HGM+ Ziru (Demon Realm)

The crown jewel of the Ghoul Battle Pass, HGM+ Ziru is a pure DPS monster. When evolved to G-rank, this Shadow deals approximately **3.5 trillion damage**—making it the highest single-target DPS unit currently available. Its signature ability, "Dark Energy Burst," delivers explosive AoE damage with a damage-over-time effect that melts through boss HP bars.

**Why it's S-Tier:**
- Highest G-rank damage potential in the game
- Dark Energy Burst excels in both single-target and AoE scenarios
- Requires Demon Realm completion to unlock, rewarding dedicated players

**Best Use Case:** Speedrunning late-game dungeons and boss fights.

### HGM+ Beru (Ant Kingdom)

The ultimate evolution of the Ant King shadow, HGM+ Beru reaches approximately **3.24 trillion damage** at G-rank. What sets this Shadow apart is its unique "Ant Swarm" ability—creating multiple ant copies that deal AoE damage across the battlefield. This makes it exceptionally powerful against waves of enemies and stationary bosses.

**Why it's S-Tier:**
- Massive AoE potential with Ant Swarm copies
- Consistent high damage output
- Spawns as G-rank variant, immediately competitive

**Best Use Case:** Dungeon farming and content with multiple enemy waves.

### HGM+ Monarch (Shadow Dungeon)

HGM+ Monarch brings a unique tactical element to combat through time manipulation. While its raw damage sits slightly below Ziru at SS-rank, its G-rank variant compensates with the devastating "Chrono Freeze" ability—temporarily freezing enemies in place and creating perfect damage windows.

**Why it's S-Tier:**
- "Chrono Freeze" provides unmatched team synergy opportunities
- Time manipulation creates combo potential with other S-Tier Shadows
- Exceptional control in PvP scenarios

**Best Use Case:** Control-oriented team compositions and PvP battles.

### Vermillion

For players who haven't unlocked the Ghoul Battle Pass rewards, Vermillion remains the gold standard for raw damage output. Its exceptional DPS makes it indispensable for late-game content, particularly in Solo City where high burst damage is essential for clearing challenging encounters.

**Why it's S-Tier:**
- Consistently highest non-HGM+ DPS
- Excels in single-target boss encounters
- Lower summoning difficulty compared to HGM+ variants

**Best Use Case:** Late-game progression and single-target scenarios.

### Longin

Longin's "Fiery Spear" attack is legendary among Arise Crossover veterans. This Shadow practically doubles your combat effectiveness with a single powerful ability. Players highly recommend stacking four copies of Longin for maximum synergy.

**Why it's S-Tier:**
- Massive damage multiplier with Fiery Spear
- Works throughout all phases of gameplay
- Four-stack strategy creates overwhelming damage

**Best Use Case:** General content clearing and boss damage.

## A-Tier Shadows — Excellent Second Choices

A-Tier Shadows form the backbone of strong team compositions. While not quite game-breaking like S-Tier, they're consistently powerful and offer excellent value for resource investment.

### Largalgan

Largalgan strikes an excellent balance between damage output and accessibility. It can be reliably farmed around the overpass near Soul City, making it an ideal choice for players building their roster. A four-stack of Largalgan provides substantial DPS, or you can try a hybrid approach with two Anders and two Largalgan.

**Why it's A-Tier:**
- Solid all-around damage stats
- Decent drop rate once you know the farming location
- Versatile team synergy options

**Best Use Case:** Mid-game progression and team building.

### Anders

Anders serves as an excellent filler Shadow, offering reliable damage that's easily accessible beneath the Largalgan overpass farming location. While it becomes less relevant at higher progression stages, Anders remains a valuable asset for early and mid-game content until you transition to endgame Shadows.

**Why it's A-Tier:**
- Easy to farm early game
- Decent damage for content clearing
- Pairs well with Largalgan for balanced teams

**Best Use Case:** Early to mid-game progression.

### Gonshee

Gonshee functions as an early-game workhorse, particularly useful for clearing starter Shadows like Soondoo. Its effectiveness diminishes once damage scaling reaches approximately 30-50, at which point it's best replaced by stronger Shadows like Longin or Anders.

**Why it's A-Tier:**
- Excellent early-game starter
- Reliable damage for beginner content
- Smooth transition to stronger Shadows

**Best Use Case:** Early game and tutorial progression.

## B-Tier Shadows — Use Temporarily, Then Upgrade

B-Tier Shadows provide temporary utility but become obsolete as your roster develops. They're worth using early but shouldn't receive significant investment.

### Daek

Daek represents a marginal improvement over Soondoo, focusing on melee combos every few seconds. It becomes irrelevant once you unlock Shadows like Longin or Largalgan, but serves as a viable placeholder during early progression.

**Why it's B-Tier:**
- Slight upgrade from starter Shadows
- Melee combo focus provides consistent damage
- Quickly outclassed by accessible alternatives

**Best Use Case:** Very early game placeholder.

### Soondoo

Every player's first Shadow, Soondoo appears at the start of your journey but has the lowest damage output in the game. Its only real value is in combining duplicates to obtain stronger Shadows. After early objectives, there's no reason to keep Soondoo in your active roster.

**Why it's B-Tier:**
- Starter Shadow available from the beginning
- Useful for Shadow fusion mechanics
- Immediate replacement recommended

**Best Use Case:** Initial fusion and early tutorials only.

## C-Tier and D-Tier Shadows — Avoid Investing

Below B-Tier, Shadows lack the damage or utility to justify investment. These units are either too weak for current content or offer extremely limited use cases. Focus your resources on S and A-Tier Shadows instead.

## PvE vs PvP Tier Differences

The tier rankings above primarily reflect **PvE performance**, which constitutes the majority of Arise Crossover's content. However, notable differences emerge in PvP scenarios:

| Aspect | PvE Priority | PvP Priority |
|--------|--------------|--------------|
| Damage Type | Sustained DPS | Burst Damage |
| Control | Less Important | Essential (HGM+ Monarch shines) |
| Healing | Helpful | Critical |
| AoE vs Single | Mix | Burst Combos |

For PvP-focused players, HGM+ Monarch's Chrono Freeze becomes significantly more valuable, potentially ranking it above Ziru in competitive matchups. Conversely, sustained DPS Shadows like Vermillion may drop in relative value when fights end quickly.

## F2P Player Recommendations

Free-to-play players should prioritize these accessible Shadows:

1. **Longin** — Highest impact Shadow available through normal farming
2. **Largalgan** — Consistent damage with easy farming location
3. **Anders** — Reliable filler until you build core team
4. **Vermillion** — Ultimate goal for sustained DPS

Save your premium currencies for HGM+ Shadow spawns or the Ghoul Battle Pass when it offers exceptional value. The four-shadow core of Longin-Largalgan-Anders-Anders can carry you through most mid-game content while you farm toward S-Tier Shadows.
