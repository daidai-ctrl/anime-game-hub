## Blox Fruits PvP Guide

Player versus Player combat is one of the most exciting aspects of Blox Fruits. Whether you're defending yourself from bounty hunters or hunting for bounty, understanding PvP mechanics is essential.

## Best PvP Builds

### Fruit Main Build
- **Stat Distribution**: Max Melee + Fruit, 1 point Defense
- **Best Fruits**: Dragon V2, Venom, Dough, Leopard
- **Accessories**: Hunter Cape, Paladin Helmet

### Sword Main Build
- **Stat Distribution**: Max Melee + Sword, 1 point Defense
- **Best Swords**: Yoru (Dark Blade), Spikey Trident, Hallow Scythe
- **Accessories**: Swordsman Hat, Dark Coat

### Hybrid Build
- **Stat Distribution**: Melee 2550, Fruit/Sword 2550, Defense 1
- **Best Setup**: Dough + Yoru, Venom + Spikey Trident

## Combat Mechanics

### I-Frames
Invincibility frames (I-frames) are crucial for PvP. Every move has startup frames where you can be hit and active frames where you're invincible. Learning I-frame timing is the key to dodging.

### Chip Damage
Small hits that add up over time. Use quick attacks between your opponent's moves to build chip damage.

### Punish Game
After blocking or dodging an attack, you have a window to punish. The heavier the attack you dodged, the bigger your punish window.

## Advanced Techniques

1. **Move Canceling** - Cancel the end lag of moves by inputting another move
2. **Ken Haki Break** - Use moves that break Observation Haki to create openings
3. **Combo Extension** - Chain moves that normally wouldn't connect by using specific timing
4. **Z-Axis Fighting** - Use verticality to avoid attacks and create angles

## Bounty Hunting Tips

- Stay in the Second Sea or Third Sea for active PvP
- Use Observation Haki (Ken) to detect nearby players
- Keep escape fruits equipped when carrying high bounty
- Hunt during peak server hours for more targets
